The quarter in brief_____________________________________________________________________________ World Wide Supply AS owns six Plattform Supply Vessels. All delivered from Damen Shipyard in the period from June 28th to December 17th 2013. During third quarter two Vessels have served a contract for GdF in the North Sea. Four Vessels are working for Petrobras in Brazil. The firm part of the Petrobras contracts are running until June 2018. The World Wide Supply Group's operating income for the third quarter was in USD, BRL and GBP equivalent to NOK 81,2 million. The EBITDA was positive with NOK 30,4 million (37,4 % EBITDA margin) and EBIT was positive with NOK 21,6 million after deduction of NOK 8,8 million in depreciation. Net financial costs was NOK 98,3 million (of which NOK 80,7 was unrealized disagio) resulting in a pre-tax loss of NOK 76,8 million.
Operations Average utilization of the fleet was 96,76% for the quarter. Four Vessels commenced their four years contract with Petrobras at the end of 2nd quarter 2014. The remaining two Vessels are on term contracts with GdF in the UK sector of the North Sea. The fixed period is expected to last until end January 2015. In addition the Charterer has an option to extend the contracts with around 180 days per Vessel.
Profit and loss Q3 2014___________________________________________________________________ Operating income was NOK 81,2 million for the 3rd quarter, while operating expenses were NOK 50,9 million. Depreciation was NOK 8,8 million. Net financial expenses were NOK 98,3 million including unrealized currency losses of net NOK 80,7 million. Net result before tax for Q3 was negative with NOK 76,7 million. There is not calculated any tax for the period. There will be no payable tax, only change in deferred tax.
Profit and loss per 30.09.2014______________________________________________________________ Operating income was NOK 165,1 million as per 3rd quarter, while operating expenses were NOK 130 million. Depreciation was NOK 26,2 million. Net financial expenses were NOK 147,6 million including unrealized currency losses of net NOK 89,3 million. Net result before tax as per Q3 was negative with NOK 138,7 million.
There is not calculated any tax for the period. There will be no payable tax, only change in deferred tax.
Balance sheet per 30.09.2014 Total assets were NOK 1 422,3 million compared to NOK 1 330 million as of 31st December 2013.

Tangible assets amounted to NOK 1 237,7 million including conversion costs for four of the group's Vessels (compared to NOK 1 234,6 million at 31.12.2013).
Based on valuations from two independant shipbrokers at 30.06.2014 the value of the Vessels were NOK 1 302,1 million. The group's current assets were NOK 155,1 million of which NOK 82,3 million in cash.
Book equity as per 30.09.2014 was NOK 441,2 million corresponding to NOK 2,90 per share while value adjusted equity was NOK 505,6 million corresponding to NOK 3,33 per share.
At the end of the quarter the Group had long term interest bearing debt in the amount of USD 150 million.
In addition there are short term interest bearing debt of EUR 1,7 million to the Yard related to Vessel conversions. This is repaid in October.
Market outlook Currently the entire World Wide Supply fleet is employed on fixed contracts. This will be the situation for the rest of 2014.
Four of the Vessels are employed on long term contracts until June 2018 and the gross value of these contracts are approximately NOK 975 million and the value of the optional periods are about NOK 1 billion. The remaining Vessels are employed until January 2015, however the Charterer GdF has options to extend the contracts for about 180 days for both Vessels.
Financial risk The Group uses financial instruments to manage its foreign currency risk in charter hire.
The Group has fixed rate on the long-term debt so there is no risk to interest rate.
The Group has an active approach to currency risk and it is entered into agreements thatreduce thisrisk. The Group's long-term debt is in US dollars. It is signed long-term time charters for four of its vessels with rate in US dollars. Payment to the company in that currency has a cash flow that covers the interest on its long-termloan inUS dollars. Therefore there is no risk associated with the payment of interest on long-term debt.
Employees____________________________________________________________________ The Group had at September 30th 184 employees of which 180 sailors including hired personnel.
