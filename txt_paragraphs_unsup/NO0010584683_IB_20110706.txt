FINANCIAL UPDATES
As communicated in our IQ report, Interoil Colombia Exploration and Production Inc. has incurred USD 12,8 million in unsecured debt due to increased cash collateral payments under the hedging agreement with Citibank and

<<<

 an extraordinary high build up of oil inventory,
This is USD 7,8 million exceeding the permitted amount under the NOK 310 million bond loan.
Interoil Colombia currently has an inventory of 60,000 barrels above normal. InterOil is presently

<<<

 delivering the oil to Hocol, a subsidiary of the state owned oil company Ecopetrol. Hocol is responsible for operating the pipelines and the off- loading facilities where our oil is being shipped out.
Interoil plans to repay USD 3 million of

<<<

 the unsecured debt by the end of week 27 on the release of the cash collateral from Citibank. The remaining USD 4,8 million will be repaid as the excess inventory is sold off. This is expected to take place during 3Q.
As of IQ Interoil Peru

<<<

 SA was in breach of the minimum average production covenant in the USD 60 million loan agreement with Citibank Peru. The minimum daily average production for the 12 month period ending on the relevant test date was 4067 BOPD. This is 727 BOPD

<<<

 below the covenant requirement of 4.800 BOPD. The shortfall was due to low production during Q2 and Q3 2010.
As of December 31, 2010, Citibank provided us with a waiver for the 4,800 barrels per day threshold, by the end of IQ Citibank has

<<<

 not issued a new waiver related to the 4,800 barrels. The bank monitors closely our drilling and production activities, and they are confident with the current production trend.
The- drilling of production wells on the San Luis Field started

<<<

 24(th) May. The first well has been put in production whilst the second well will be placed in production by mid July. According to the production plan for Peru do we expect to raise the production to above 4700 BOPD by the end of July.
