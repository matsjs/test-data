9.1 Interest accrues from and including the Disbursement Date:
(b) from (and including) the Former Maturity Date, at a fixed rate of 5.00 percentage points per annum.
The interest is paid in arrears on each Interest Payment Date, the first Interest Payment Date falling in May 2007.
On each Interest Payment Date, the Borrower shall pay accrued interest due on such Interest Payment Date in cash using Available Cash. To the extent that the Borrower determines that there is insufficient Available Cash to pay all or part of the accrued

interest payable on the relevant Interest Payment Date and shall not be paying all or part of the accrued interest due on an Interest Payment Date in cash, the Borrower shall:
(i) not later than four Banking Days before the relevant Interest Payment Date, issue a notice to the Loan Trustee (for immediate distribution to the Bondholders) and the paying agent appointed in connection with the Bonds at the time confirming the amount (if any) of interest that is to be paid in cash and the amount of interest that is to be paid by way of PIK Interest (as defined and described below), and providing a detailed calculation of the Cash and Available Cash in the Group; and
(ii) on the relevant Interest Payment Date, satisfy its payment obligations with respect to the portion of the accrued interest that cannot be paid in cash due to insufficient Available Cash (such amount, the "Unpaid Accrued Interest") by the issue of additional Bonds (the "PIK Interest") by the Borrower to the Bondholders on a pro rata basis in amount equivalent to (a) the Unpaid Accrued Interest plus (b) the amount of additional interest that would have accrued on that portion of the Remaining Loan to which the Unpaid Accrued Interest relates since the last Interest Payment Date had the interest rate during that period been (A) from (and including) the Disbursement Date to (and excluding) the Former Maturity Date, LIBOR plus the applicable PIK Margin; or (B) from (and including) the Former Maturity Date, a fixed rate of 7.00 percentage points per annum.
For avoidance of doubt, the issue of additional Bonds in payment of PIK Interest shall be taken into account for the purposes of calculating the amount of PIK Interest that is payable on the Remaining Loan at each following Interest Payment Date.
