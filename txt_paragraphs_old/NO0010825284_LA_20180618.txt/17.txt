"Permitted Financial Indebtedness" means, subject always to compliance with Clause 13.15 (a) (Financial Covenants and Incurrence Test):
(b) the Existing Debt, provided that:
(i) the Seller's Credit is repaid (and the Seller's Credit Guarantee and any related security, including the Cash Deposit, if applicable, is released in full) no later than the date falling three months after the Issue Date; and
(ii) pending such repayment and release, the security for the Seller's Credit Guarantee is released in full and replaced with the Cash Deposit no later than through or in connection with the Initial Disbursement in accordance with the Closing Procedure;
(c) any Shareholder Loans; and
(d) any Financial Indebtedness arising as a result of a contemplated refinancing of the Bonds in full provided that (i) the Issuer has served a call notice in respect of all Outstanding Bonds, in accordance with Clause 10.2 (c); and (ii) the net proceeds from the issuance of such Financial Indebtedness is held in escrow until the Bonds have been repaid in full.
