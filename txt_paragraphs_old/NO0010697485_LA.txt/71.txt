14.1 Release o f Subsidiary Guarantors
The Issuer may request that a Subsidiary Guarantor ceases to be a Subsidiary Guarantor, by delivering to the Bond Trustee a release request, if a Subsidiary Guarantor is no longer a Vessel Owner or a Charterer and the Issuer has confirmed to the Bond Trustee that this is the case.
14.1.2 The Bond Trustee shall accept a release request pursuant to Clause 14.1.1 and notify the Issuer of its acceptance if:
(i)
no payment is due from the Subsidiary Guarantor under Bond Subsidiary
Guarantee;
(ii) all material assets of the Subsidiary Guarantor to be released have been transferred to another Subsidiary Guarantor or the Subsidiary Guarantor to be released is liquidated as soon as practically possible following such release (with any surplus assets being distributed to its shareholders which are also Subsidiary Guarantors) in each case as confirmed by the auditor of the Issuer or such Subsidiary Guarantor; and
(iii) the Issuer has confirmed that no Event of Default is continuing or would result from the acceptance of the release request.
