"Market Loan" means any loan or other indebtedness where an entity issues commercial paper, certificates, subordinated debentures, bonds or any other debt securities (including, for the avoidance of doubt, medium term note programmes and other market funding programmes), provided in each case that such instruments and securities are or can be subject to trade on a regulated or unregulated recognised market place.
"Material Adverse Effect" means a material adverse effect on (a) the business, financial condition or operations of the Group taken as a whole, (b) the Group's ability to perform and comply with the Finance Documents, or (c) the validity or enforceability of the Finance Documents.
"Material Group Company" means the Issuer or a Group Company representing more than 10.00 per cent, of the Total Assets or EBITDA of the Group on a consolidated basis according to the latest Financial Report.
"Material Intercompany Loan" means any future intercompany loan between the Issuer as lender and a Subsidiary as borrower with a tenor in excess of six months or that has been outstanding for more than six months, in an amount exceeding EUR 1,000,000 or the equivalent in any other currency.
"Net Finance Charges" means, for the Relevant Period, the aggregate amount of the accrued interest, commission, fees, discounts, payment fees, premiums or charges and other finance payments in respect of Financial Indebtedness whether paid or payable by any member of the Group according to the latest Financial Report(s) (calculated on a consolidated basis) other than Transaction Costs, after deducting any interest payable for that Relevant Period to any member of the Group and any interest income relating to cash.
