"Change of Control Period" means a period of 60 days following the Bond Trustee's receipt of notice from the Ultimate Parent that a Change of Control Event has occurred.
"Costs" means all costs, expenses, disbursements, payments, charges, losses, demands, claims, liabilities, penalties, fines, damages, judgments, orders, sanctions, fees (including travel expenses, VAT, court fees and legal fees) and any other outgoings of whatever nature.
"Day Count Fraction" means the day count fraction specified in clause 9.4.
"Earnings" means all (i) hire payable to the Issuer or the Parent, respectively, for the use of either of the Vessels under charter parties or other contracts of employment, the duration of which is 12 months or longer (ii) compensation payable to the Issuer or the Parent in the event of requisition of either of the Vessels, (iii) remuneration for salvage and other services performed by either of the Vessels payable to the Issuer or the Parent (iv) demurrage and retention moneys receivable by the Issuer or the Parent in relation to the Vessels under charter parties or other contracts of employment, the duration of which is 12 months or longer, (v) damage for breach (or payments for variation or termination) of any contract of employment of either of the Vessels the duration of which is 12 months or longer, and (vi) other monies whatsoever due or to become due to the Issuer or the Parent from any third parties or any Group Company in respect of the Vessels or otherwise.
"Earnings Account" means certain accounts with Skandinaviska Enskilda Banken AB, Oslo Branch, pledged (but not blocked) in favour of the Bond Trustee (on behalf of the Bondholders), in which the bank operating the account has waived any set-off rights.
"Earnings Account Pledge" means a second priority pledge over the Parent's and the Issuer's respective claims against the relevant banks for the amounts from time to time standing to the credit of the Parent and Issuer respectively in the Earnings Accounts, ranking after the relevant First Security.
