The following summary of the Perpetual Notes contains basic information about the Perpetual Notes. It is not intended to be complete and it is subject to important limitations and exceptions. For a more complete understanding of the Perpetual Notes, including certain definitions of terms used in this summary, see "Terms and Conditions o f the Perpetual Notes'".
Notes Offered................................ Perpetual Notes due 2115. Notes Issue Date............................ Expected to be on or about February 9, 2016. Notes Maturity Date..................... December 30, 2115. Interest Rate .................................. 2.0% per annum. Interest Payment Dates; Deferral . Subject to the provisions of the Perpetual Notes relating to deferral of interest
payments at the sole discretion of the Parent, interest on the Perpetual Notes will be paid annually in arrears on each December 30, commencing December 30, 2016, and will accrue from the Issue Date. The Parent may in its sole discretion defer the payment of interest on the Perpetual Notes, in whole or in part, by delivering deferral notices to holders. The Parent is not subject to the any limits as to the number of times the interest payments and deferred interest payments can be deferred. A deferred interest payment may, at the option of the Parent, be paid at any time. Denomination ................................ Each Perpetual Note will be issued in global registered form (Regulation S and Rule 144A global notes) and have a minimum denomination of 100,000 and integral multiples of 1.00 in excess thereof. Perpetual Notes in denominations of less than 100,000 will not be available. Ranking of the Perpetual Notes .... The Perpetual Notes will:  be deeply subordinated unsecured subordinated obligations of the Parent;  rank pari passu in right of payment with any existing and future subordinated
indebtedness of the Parent that is not subordinated in right of payment to the Perpetual Notes;  rank junior in right of payment to any existing and future senior and subordinated indebtedness of the Parent except such indebtedness is expressly pari passu with or subordinated to the Perpetual Notes;  be effectively subordinated to any existing or future indebtedness or obligation of the Parent that is secured by property and assets that do not secure the Perpetual Notes, to the extent of the value of the property and assets securing such indebtedness; and  be structurally subordinated to any existing or future indebtedness of the Subsidiaries of the Parent, including obligations to trade creditors. Guarantees.................................... The Perpetual Notes will not be guaranteed. Security.......................................... The Perpetual Notes will be unsecured obligations of the Parent. Optional Redemption ................... The Parent may redeem all or part of the Perpetual Notes at any time on or after December 30, 2045 at the redemption prices described under "Terms and Conditions o f the Perpetual Notes--Redemption and Purchase", in each case, plus accrued and unpaid interest and additional amounts, if any, to the date of redemption. Prior to December 30, 2045, the Parent may redeem all or part of the Perpetual Notes at a redemption price equal to 100% of the principal amount thereof, plus accrued and unpaid interest, if any, plus the applicable "make-whole" premium. The Parent may also redeem in whole, but not in part, the Perpetual Notes at the specified redemption price, (i) if the Parent experiences a change of control or (ii) upon the occurrence of certain tax events, rating events or accounting events.
