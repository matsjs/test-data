The proceeds from the issuance of the Bonds shall be held by the Arranger on the Escrow Accounts and may only be released when the conditions precedent for disbursement of the Net Proceeds have been fulfilled pursuant to Clause 4.2 below.
(a) The Trustee's approval of the disbursement from the Escrow Accounts of the Net Proceeds is subject to the following documents being received by the Trustee, in form and substance satisfactory to it (acting reasonably), and that the following actions have been taken or will occur on the disbursement date:
(i) confirmation from the Arranger that a sufficient amount of Bonds have been subscribed for (either in or by a combination of (i) cash or (ii) conversion of existing loans under the Existing Bridge Loan to Bonds) in order for the Issuer to be able to discharge the Existing Bridge Loan on the disbursement date (by a combination of (i) cash payments or (ii) set-off against Bonds or (iii) set-off against bonds issued by Concent Utveckling Holding 3 AB (publ) which have been purchased by the Issuer with part of the Net Proceeds);
(ii) confirmation from the Arranger that the Transactions Costs have or will be paid on the date of disbursement;
(iii) duly executed corporate authorization documents, issued by any party that shall execute a Finance Document;
(iv) duly executed copies of the Finance Documents, including fulfilment of the relevant perfection requirements as set out therein;
(v) confirmation that the Bonds have been registered with the CSD; and
(vi) evidence that the acquisition of the Property Company by Midco has or will be completed on the date of disbursement of the Net Proceeds.
(b) When the conditions precedent for disbursement set out in Clause 4.2(a) have been fulfilled to the satisfaction of the Trustee (acting reasonably) or waived by the Trustee, the Trustee shall notify the Arranger that the Net Proceeds may be released from the Escrow Accounts, to be applied as set out in Clause 3 (Purpose of the Bonds) and in accordance with the instructions of the Arranger.
