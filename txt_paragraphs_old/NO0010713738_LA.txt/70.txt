Finance Charges mean, for the Relevant Period, the aggregate amount of the accrued interest, commission, fees, discounts, payment fees, premiums or charges and other finance payments in respect of Borrowings by any Group Company according to the latest Financial Report(s) (calculated on a consolidated basis) without taking into account a) any Transaction Costs or any capitalised interest and b) any costs related to performance bonds, bid bonds and similar instruments.
Interest Coverage Ratio means the ratio of EBITDA for the Group (on a consolidated basis) to the Net Finance Charge for the Group (on a consolidated basis).
Leverage Ratio means the ratio of Net Interest Bearing Debt to EBITDA for the Group (on a consolidated basis).
Net Finance Charges mean, for the Relevant Period, the Finance Charges according to the latest Financial Report(s), after deducting any interest payable for that Relevant Period to any Group Company and any interest income relating to Free Cash.
Net Interest Bearing Debt means Borrowings less Free Cash.

Relevant Period means each period of 12 months ending on the last day of the Issuer's financial year and each period of 12 months ending on the last day of each financial quarterly period of the Issuer's financial year.
Transaction Costs means all fees, costs and expenses incurred by a Group Company in connection with (a) the establishment of the Bond Issue, (b) the establishment of the SSRCF, (c) the repayment of the Existing Indebtedness and (d) the listing of the Bonds on the Exchange.
