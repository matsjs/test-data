"Seisranger Asset Pledge" means a first priority assignment and/or pledge in favour of the BondTrustee (on behalf of the Bondholders) of (i) any Insurances related to Seisranger, (ii) the Seisranger Earnings Account, (iii) Seisranger AS's right under the relevant Existing Internal Loans and (iv) any trade receivables of Seisranger AS (No: factoringpant).
"Seisranger Assignment of Charterparties and Earnings" means the first priority assignment in favour of the Bond Trustee (on behalf of the Bondholders) of any Charter relating to Seisranger, from time to time, and any earnings thereunder owed by the relevant Client to Seisranger AS.
"Seisranger Distributions" shall have the meaning ascribed to it in Clause 14.5.5(d).
"Seisranger Earnings Account" means (i) the account opened and maintained in the name of Seisranger AS into which all earnings related to Seisranger shall be paid, (ii) if any such earnings are received in more than one currency, any additional accounts opened in the applicable currency.
"Share Pledges" means the first priority pledges in favour of the Bond Trustee (on behalf of the Bondholders) over alt the shares of (i) the Issuer, (ii) Seisranger AS and (iii) (if applicable) the Internal Bareboat Charterer.
"Subordinated Loan" means any loan(s) provided by the Parent to the Issuer, Seisranger AS and/or the Internal Bareboat Charterer (if applicable), which is subordinated in rank to the Bonds and governed by a Subordinated Loan Agreement.
"Subordinated Loan Agreement" means a loan agreement which shall be in form and substance satisfactory to the Bond Trustee and reflect or incorporate the terms of the Subordination Agreement.
"Subordination Agreement" means the agreement to be entered into between the Issuer, the Issuer's Subsidiary, the Parent, Seisranger AS, the Vessel Manager, the Internal Bareboat Charterer (if applicable) and the Bond Trustee regarding coordination of rights and remedies generally and specifically in relation to the Management Agreement and any Subordinated Loan Agreement(s).
