10.5 Mandatory early redemption at the Longstop Date (a) If the funds deposited on the Escrow Accounts have not in all material respects been released from the Escrow Accounts and applied as set out in the Clause 2.3 (Use o f Proceeds) by the Longstop Date, the Issuer shall no later than 5 Business Days thereafter (with the Longstop Date being the Record Date) or at the sole option of the Issuer earlier if it becomes evident that the Minority Acquisition will not be completed, redeem Bonds and the Temporary Bonds at a price equal to 101.00% of the Nominal Amount (plus accrued and unpaid interest on the Bonds to be redeemed).
(b) The Issuer shall have the right to repay Temporaty Bonds with Roll-Over Bonds (as payment in kind to the holders of the Temporary Bonds (valued at par value)). Any accrued and unpaid interest on the Temporary Bonds, call premium or other amounts payable in respect of any mandatory redemption under this Clause 10.5, shall be payable in cash, provided however, that the Issuer is entitled to withhold (by set-off) the 1.00% premium payable in respect o f any mandatory redemption under this Clause 10.5 and any Roll-Over Bonds to cover reimbursement of the 2.50% premium originally paid to the Existing NCG Bondholders on the Issue Date.
10.6 Voluntary Partial Repayment The Issuer may repay an amount not exceeding 10% of the sum of the Initial Loan Amount plus any additional Bonds issued on an Interest Payment Date in any 12 month period from (but excluding) the Issue Date to (but excluding) the First Call Date (without carry-back or carry forward), at the price applicable at the First Call Date of the total aggregate Nominal Amount of the Bonds redeemed together with accrued and unpaid interest on such Bonds.
