Russia
We have been advised by our Russian counsel that Russian courts will generally recognize the mutual agreement of parties on the choice of a foreign court as an appropriate forum. However, Russian state commercial (``arbitrazhny") courts will generally not allow the initiation of legal proceedings in Russia if proceedings including the same dispute between the same parties have been commenced in a foreign court. By contrast, Russian courts of general jurisdiction (which decide cases involving physical persons and not related to entrepreneurial activities) will refuse to initiate proceedings in Russia only if the decision of a foreign court may be enforced in Russia or there is a treaty on recognition of foreign court decisions. Moreover, Russian courts (both commercial courts and courts of general jurisdiction) will allow parallel legal proceedings in Russia if the matter falls within the exclusive jurisdiction of Russian courts, such as cases concerning the title to real estate located in Russia, intellectual property rights registered in Russia, or the establishment, registration and liquidation of Russian legal entities or decisions of their management bodies.
